    What Are Mortgages?

    A mortgage is a loan specifically designed for purchasing real estate. When you take out a mortgage, you borrow money from a lender (like Light Bank) to buy a home, and in return, you agree to repay the loan over a specified period, typically ranging from 15 to 30 years. Mortgages usually come with lower interest rates compared to other types of loans, making them an accessible way to finance your property.

    What Are Savings Accounts?

    A savings account is a deposit account offered by banks that allows you to earn interest on your money while keeping it safe and easily accessible. Unlike checking accounts, which are primarily used for everyday transactions, savings accounts are ideal for setting aside funds for future needs. They often come with lower withdrawal limits, encouraging you to save rather than spend.
